# Project Idea Proposals

## 1. Social Rehearsal Studio

### One-Line Definition
A conversation rehearsal service that helps users practice difficult real-life conversations before they happen.

### Problem
People feel significant stress before conversations such as resigning, setting boundaries, asking for refunds, apologizing, or giving feedback. Search results rarely fit their exact situation, and practicing with another person can feel awkward or unavailable.

### Target Users
- Office workers
- Job seekers
- University students
- Service workers
- People who struggle with difficult conversations

### Core Value Proposition
Users can rehearse before the real moment, refine wording to be softer or firmer, and enter the real conversation with more clarity and emotional stability.

### Core Features
- Situation templates: resignation, refund, refusal, feedback, apology, boundary setting
- Role-based simulation with likely counterpart responses
- User response input via text or voice
- Feedback engine for tone and clarity
- Alternative phrasing suggestions with different tones

### MVP Scope
- Text-based simulation
- 8-12 high-value situation templates
- 3 tone modes: soft, balanced, firm
- Saveable summary with recommended phrasing

### Excluded From V1
- Real-time voice conversation
- Avatars or video
- Multiplayer roleplay
- Long-term personalized training

### Differentiation
This is not a generic chatbot. It is a practice space specialized for emotionally difficult conversations.

### Monetization Potential
- Premium scenario packs
- Subscription by category such as career, leadership, or relationships
- Enterprise communication training version

### Risk
If the scenario writing and feedback quality are weak, the product may feel like a toy.

### Why It Is Strong Now
The demo is compelling, the problem is easy to understand, and the AI use case has a clear product purpose.

## 2. The Tiny Audience

### One-Line Definition
A micro-publishing service where users share unfinished work with a small trusted audience instead of broadcasting to everyone.

### Problem
People often want feedback on early drafts but hesitate because public platforms feel too exposed and performance-driven. Existing social platforms favor finished work, while collaboration tools often feel too formal or work-oriented.

### Target Users
- Writers
- Designers
- Developers
- Indie makers
- Students
- Hobbyist creators

### Core Value Proposition
Users can share early ideas with low social pressure and receive the kind of feedback they actually want, reducing the emotional barrier to creating in public.

### Core Features
- Draft posting for text, images, links, and short context
- Small audience sharing with 3-10 invitees
- Feedback type selection such as encouragement, critique, direction check, or naming help
- Structured feedback forms
- Version comparison across drafts

### MVP Scope
- Text and image draft uploads
- Invite-only or private-link sharing
- Feedback request tags
- Response collection view
- Basic version history

### Excluded From V1
- Public feed
- Recommendation algorithms
- Large community features
- Complex permission models

### Differentiation
This is neither a social network nor a document tool. It is built specifically for sharing unfinished work.

### Monetization Potential
- Pro plan for more projects and stronger version history
- Small-team plans
- Group features for creator communities

### Risk
Without a small trusted network, the product can lose much of its value.

### Why It Is Strong Now
Creator fatigue with public posting is real, so the product philosophy is timely and brandable.

## 3. Decision Replay

### One-Line Definition
A service that helps people review whether their decisions were good in hindsight and learn their own judgment patterns over time.

### Problem
Individuals and teams make important decisions regularly, but rarely compare their original reasoning with actual outcomes in a structured way. As a result, the same judgment mistakes repeat.

### Target Users
- Solo builders
- Team leads
- Startups
- Product managers
- Individuals making career or life decisions

### Core Value Proposition
Users can examine how they think, improve how they make choices, and create a record of reasoning instead of relying only on memory or intuition.

### Core Features
- Decision cards with problem, options, reasoning, assumptions, and confidence
- Scheduled follow-up check-ins
- Outcome entry after time passes
- Prediction versus reality comparison
- Pattern summaries such as overconfidence, missing information, or rushed judgment

### MVP Scope
- Personal decision cards
- Reminder for later review
- Outcome comparison screen
- Tag and category filters
- Simple pattern statistics

### Excluded From V1
- Team comments
- Voting
- Slack or email integrations
- Advanced analytics

### Differentiation
This is not just a note-taking tool. It is a judgment-improvement product.

### Monetization Potential
- Pro plan for individuals
- Team retrospective boards
- Coaching templates for leadership and decision frameworks

### Risk
It may feel too serious or rigid if the experience is not designed with warmth and reflection in mind.

### Why It Is Strong Now
The structure is solid, the MVP is straightforward to build, and it can expand to both personal and team use cases.

## 4. Micro-Fandom Quest App

### One-Line Definition
A taste-driven quest app that turns ordinary walks and city movement into collectible missions based on niche interests.

### Problem
Daily movement through cities often becomes repetitive and numb. People want small moments of discovery and play, but most products are too general and not tuned to personal taste.

### Target Users
- Walk lovers
- Urban explorers
- Casual photographers
- Niche-interest communities
- Travelers

### Core Value Proposition
Users can transform ordinary movement into a personal exploration game shaped by their own taste and curiosity.

### Core Features
- Quest themes such as strange signs, old tiles, brutalist buildings, or cat traces
- Discovery logging with photo, location, and short notes
- Collection board
- Seasonal or location-based missions
- Browsing discoveries from others

### MVP Scope
- 5-8 quest themes
- Photo uploads
- Location tags
- Collection progress display
- Seeded demo missions or sample city data

### Excluded From V1
- Real-time community features
- Advanced map exploration
- Ranking systems
- Complex local recommendation engines

### Differentiation
This is neither productivity software nor a general social platform. It is a taste-based real-world exploration product.

### Monetization Potential
- Premium quest packs
- Local curation partnerships
- Brand or exhibition collaborations for seasonal missions

### Risk
The concept is exciting at first, but long-term retention needs careful design.

### Why It Is Strong Now
It is visually distinctive, highly memorable, and excellent for a strong portfolio-grade demo.

## Quick Comparison

- Most practical: `Decision Replay`
- Most original: `Micro-Fandom Quest App`
- Strongest emotional pull: `Social Rehearsal Studio`
- Strongest branding potential: `The Tiny Audience`

## Recommended Priority

1. `Social Rehearsal Studio`
2. `The Tiny Audience`
3. `Decision Replay`
4. `Micro-Fandom Quest App`
